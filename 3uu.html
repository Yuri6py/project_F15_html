The F-15E STRIKE EAGLE

In a world of military aviation, one aircraft rules supreme. Built to put American pilots back in charge of the skies.

This is an ultimate air superiority fighter

Development

F-111F

In the early 1980s, the U.S. Air Force sought a replacement for the General Dynamics F-111, aiming for an aircraft capable of both air superiority and ground attack missions. McDonnell Douglas proposed the F-15E, an evolution of the F-15 Eagle, incorporating advanced avionics, increased fuel capacity, and the ability to carry a wide array of precision-guided munitions. The first production F-15E made its maiden flight on December 11, 1986, and the aircraft entered service with the USAF in 1988.

Features

The F-15E Strike Eagle is equipped with advanced radar and electronic systems, making it a highly capable multi-role fighter. Central to its capabilities is the AN/APG-70 radar, which provides long-range detection, terrain-following, and precision targeting. This radar enables the aircraft to track multiple targets simultaneously and engage both air and ground threats with precision-guided munitions. Additionally, the F-15E is outfitted with the LANTIRN targeting and navigation pods, allowing for low-altitude, night, and all-weather operations. Coupled with its advanced electronic warfare suite, including radar warning receivers and countermeasure dispensers, the Strike Eagle maintains situational awareness and survivability in contested environments.

Service history

The F-15C and F-15E have demonstrated exceptional service records, becoming legends in military aviation. The F-15C, optimized for air superiority, achieved an unprecedented 104:0 kill ratio, securing its dominance in aerial combat. Notable victories include its role in Operation Desert Storm, where it downed numerous enemy aircraft, including MiG-29s and Su-22s. In a remarkable demonstration of versatility, an F-15C successfully destroyed a satellite in low Earth orbit during the 1985 ASM-135 ASAT test, showcasing its ability to adapt to unconventional missions. The F-15E Strike Eagle excelled in both air-to-air and air-to-ground roles, proving its versatility during the same conflict by delivering precision strikes on strategic targets while also engaging enemy aircraft. Over decades of service, both variants have earned a reputation for reliability, effectiveness, and unmatched combat performance.

Armament

The F-15E can be equiped with the following list of weapon

Air to air weapon

AIM-120 AMRAAM

The AIM-120 AMRAAM is a radar-guided missile designed for beyond-visual-range combat. With active radar homing and compatibility with various aircraft, it delivers unmatched accuracy and reliability, making it a cornerstone of modern air combat.

AIM-9M

The AIM-9M Sidewinder is a heat-seeking, short-range air-to-air missile known for its reliability and versatility. With advanced infrared tracking and countermeasure resistance, it excels in dogfights and remains a key weapon in modern air combat.

Air to ground weapon

AGM-65

The AGM-65 Maverick is a precision-guided, air-to-ground missile designed for close air support and strike missions. Known for its versatility, it uses electro-optical or infrared guidance to target armored vehicles, ships, and fortified structures with pinpoint accuracy. Widely deployed, the AGM-65 is a trusted weapon for diverse combat scenarios.

GBU

The F-15E Strike Eagle carries various Guided Bomb Units (GBUs) for precision strikes. These include laser-guided GBU-10 and GBU-12 Paveways for pinpoint accuracy, GPS-guided GBU-31 and GBU-38 JDAMs for all-weather operations, and GBU-39 SDBs for high-impact, low-collateral strikes. This versatility allows it to excel in diverse missions.

AGM-154 Joint Standoff Weapon

The AGM-154 Joint Standoff Weapon (JSOW) is a precision-guided glide bomb designed for long-range strikes. Using GPS and inertial navigation, it delivers high accuracy against stationary and moving targets. The F-15E can deploy the AGM-154 to neutralize bunkers, air defenses, and other high-value targets, ensuring effectiveness in complex combat scenarios.
